No Jobs

Labor market intelligence for displaced workers.

A data product for people whose line of work is being eroded or eliminated by AI. You want to know what’s still paying in your metro. We surface it.

What each issue covers

  • Regional hiring surface — current openings in your metro, sorted by pay, credential requirements, and remote availability.
  • AI-risk flags on every posting — green for structurally insulated, yellow for reshaping, red for contracting.
  • Protected-category spotlight — jobs that are both hiring and insulated from AI: regulated trades, licensure-gated white-collar, touch healthcare, local services.
  • Employer instability signal — WARN notices and SEC filings naming the employers about to shed workers near you.

Editorial premise

When labor-saving technology lets two workers do the work of ten, firms keep two and book the margin. Credentials don’t protect a category from that pressure. The product helps readers navigate the situation honestly — which categories are contracting, which adjacent categories are not, and what a realistic pivot actually costs.

Status

Early development. First metro: Chicago (Illinois-side — Cook, DuPage, Lake, Will, Kane, McHenry counties). Additional metros follow.

Data sources

SEC EDGAR 8-K filings, Illinois WARN Act notices, O*NET occupational data, CareerOneStop labor market information, and supplemental job-posting feeds. Government statistical data is used for context; private and event-driven sources carry the primary signal.
